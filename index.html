AI Curb Appeal (Coming Soon)

Next.js, NoSQL, CSS

Operating on a subscription model, the site helps homeowners and property owners save money on costly design changes by visualizing updates before committing. The project has been a rewarding challenge so far as my first major venture with Next.js. From integrating multiple AI APIs and managing NoSQL databases to handling Stripe payments, it has pushed me to learn new technologies and how to interconnect them to all work together.

The user is given the ability to upload images of their our and train an AI model on it. Using text prompts on their new model, the user can change anything about their home including color, material, structure, and landscaping

Securely loging in gives users the ability to navigate to the dashboard and have full control over what model they run, text prompt to alter the image, "negative" prompts or things the model should avoid making, and how strictly the model should stick to the images it was trained on.

Once a model is choosen and prompt is given, the model gets to work creating 4 images following the users text. All of this is sent off to our database and the list is updated once the images come back from the model.

Though this project has a little ways to go until I can release it I wanted to share where my progress is at and what ability I have been able to give to users. Stay tuned as I will keep this site up to date when this project is released!

Copaneazi's Pizzeria

CMS, CSS

While it might seem unconventional to include a CMS site in the projects section of a developer portfolio, I wanted to highlight the various skills involved in building a website. This project allowed me to dive into several aspects, such as creating images in Photoshop, photographing products, and gathering information that the client wanted displayed—like current menu items and pricing. I also focused on integrating their ordering system and optimizing for SEO.

Working with clients whose entire livelihood revolves around the business we’re building the site for can be intense, but I found it incredibly engaging and collaborative. I loved taking their ideas and adding my unique twists to bring the project to life.

Getting the right image for the hero section was essential for capturing users' interest. It was also important to include what 90% of people are looking for right away. The client wanted users to be able to immediately call or place an order.

Demonstrating the considerable amount of effort and dedication that they invest into every single aspect of their restaurant was a crucial and pivotal element of the project.

Given that this restaurant currently operates two locations, it was important for the client to emphasize the various methods available for contacting each location, making this a key focus of the project.

It was important to me to present their product in an engaging and playful manner that truly makes it stand out and pop off the page. Utilizing the widely popular "masonry" layout effectively contributes to achieving that vibrant and dynamic feeling.

The client had an image that perfectly captures their fun attitude and emphasizes that the business is locally owned by an individual, not a national corporation. I enjoyed taking their image, editing it, and incorporating it at the bottom of every page to maintain a cohesive look.

As I mentioned, this site was built using a low-code platform, where my main focus was on custom CSS. This project underscores the crucial role of gathering and creating images that align with the client's vision and overall feel. It's vital for a developer to consider elements like achieving the right atmosphere, ensuring smooth navigation, optimizing for SEO, and effectively showcasing products—all essential tools in their toolkit.

The Synth Website

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Three.js

The Synth Website was an end of trimester project focused on front-end technologies, where my partner and I explored 3D design. The site places users in a cabin with a functional synthesizer on a table, overlooking a mountain landscape. Users can create and loop synth sequences alongside a drum pad with instruments like kick drum, hi-hat, and snare. A dropdown menu lets users modify the outdoor scene, adding snow or rain and switching from daylight to an amber evening.

The steep learning curve of this project centered around using libraries like Three.js and Tone.js to build the 3D environment and make the objects functional. While load times can sometimes feel like waiting for dial-up, I’m thrilled with how the site turned out. The unique challenges pushed us to think creatively.
